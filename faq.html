How was Ali’s upbringing before fame?
Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. was born on January 17, 1942 in Louisville,
Kentucky during the civil rights movement. Ali was well acquainted to
being exposed to racism from an early age. Ate age 12, as a result of
having his bicycled stolen and notifying authorities, policeman Joe
Martin invited him to a boxing lesson at a recreational center and
observed his potential.
What obstacles did Ali face?
Growing up in the 1940’s Ali was subjected to racial inequality. Ali
was dyslexic and this didn’t come to his attention later on in his
life. Converting to Islam and his adherence towards his beliefs led
him to advocate for the Islamic community.
What are some interesting things about Ali?
Aside of his physical prowess, Ali was known for his rhyme scheme as
he would trash talk his opponents and was sometimes considered the
first rapper. Ali knew Malcom X personally as he explored into the world of Islam.
How was Ali an influential figure in the African American community during the Civil rights Movements
Ali refused to be inducted into the US Army to fight the Vietnam war
and was a pioneer of counter culture. He challenged white supremacy
and had racial pride. Ali went on to several colleges and HBCUs and
talked about the importance of education.
What are some examples of Alis philanthropic contributions?
Ali donated to a number of non-profit organizations that were targeted
towards bettering the world not just for the African American community
but others as well. Ali made one of the largest donations to the United
Negro College Fund. Ali supported relief and development initiatives in
Africa and Asia.
What legal controversies did Ali face in his career?
As a result of refusing to be inducted in the Vietnam war, Ali he eventually
was arrested then went to court for this. In, addition to this he was stripped
of his boxing licenses and couldn’t fight legally. Along with other influential
figures such as Martin Luther King Jr, Howard Baker and journalists among others,
the NSA and FBI monitored Alis communications.
What legacy does Ali leave behind?
Ali's life left behind legacies that span multiple layers. There are numerous
statues and sculptures in his image in many places in America and elsewhere.
In addition to what his philanthropic efforts resulted to, Alis charitable work
provided foundation for causes that were for the greater good. The numerous
literature and media about him will definitely ensure his name is never forgotten.
Ali had many children and grand children some of which both became professional
fighters. There are many sites of interest of him in Louisville Kentucky.
What are some of the accolades Ali was awarded?
Ali has received numerous accolades from a variety of organizations. Of these
include the National Golden Gloves light heavy-weight Champion award, World
Heavyweight champion, Dr. Martin Luther King Memorial Award. Honorary Doctorate
of Humane Letters, Texas Southern University, being elected to boxing Hall of
Fame, United Nations Messager of Peace Award. Ali has far more awards than I
can even list. In addition, he has a street named after him in Phoenix, Arizona.
